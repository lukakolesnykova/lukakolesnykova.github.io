Projects > Strykk

Collaborative project with Ivy Samuel.

Problem: Non-alcoholic spirits, including Strykk, often don't taste great on their own. This negative reputation around taste can deter potential consumers.

Insight: People enjoy well-made drinks and the experience of discovering new cocktails. The real enjoyment of spirits, alcoholic or not, comes from how they are mixed. By focusing on cocktails, Strykk can turn its perceived weakness into a strength.

Solution: Adding some "spirit" to Dry January by letting people discover their personalized Strykk cocktail ‚Äì because you are what you drink!

Introducing a new Strykk cocktail menu, loosely inspired by the Myers‚ÄìBriggs personalities.

Launch of ads to showcase the new menu, by inviting people to take an online quiz to help people discover their personalized Strykk cocktail.

Installing vending machines in public places where people can take the quiz and sample their cocktail and provide receipts to allow reordering of your cocktail in participating bars.

Offering online Strykk cocktail boxes, allowing people to make their Strykk cocktail from home.

Integrate Strykk cocktails into The Sims 4, allowing players to host in-game Strykk events and participate in virtual cocktail readings.

Projects > Falmouth Food Co-op

Brief: The Falmouth Food Co-Op is a community run online grocery store, with products from local non-industrial farmers. During a talk, they mentioned people are reluctant to place orders due to the difficulty of using their website. However, they stated they were unable to remake their website due to the backend.

Solution: Creating an easy to use app prototype to simplify the order process.

App sitemap based on their current website, and simplifying it to better suit an app.

Lo-fi wireframe & diagram for main app screens.
